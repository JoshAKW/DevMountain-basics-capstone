View Possible Destinations

Olympus Mons, an extreme extinct volcano about the size of arizona and nearly 16 miles in elevation.
Tharsis Volcanoes, hosts 12 gigantic volcanoes in a zone roughly 2500 miles wide.
Valles Marineris, the largest canyon in the solar system. It is almost 4 times longer than the grand canyon, at 1850 miles.
The North and South Poles, with strong winds and the only visible ice on the planet.













Gale Crater and Mount Sharp(Aeolis Mons) The curirosity rover lander here in 2012 There is evidence of old water flows and organic molecules within the nearly 3.5 billion old rocks strewn around the area.
Medusae Fossae, Consider by some conspiracy theorists to be a "ufo" crash site, it is more likely that these are a ancient volcanic deposit nearly a 1/5th the size of the U.S.A.
Recurring Slope Linae in Hale Crater, still boggling the minds of nasa scientists, the RSL appear to be mars version of crystallized sand dunes, but we aren't sure how they were formed. Mystery abounds!
Ghost Dunes in Noctis Labyrinthus and Hellas basin. These regions used to hold dunes that were 10's of meters tall but were later eroded by lava or water. These dunes show how winds used to flow on ancient mars, allowing you to imagine when mars whens it climate was lush and vibrant.
